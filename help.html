Frequently Asked Questions

10 questions
How do I create a news alert?
To create a news alert, navigate to the Alerts page using the sidebar, then click the "Create Alert" button. Fill in the alert name, select a category and source, and add keywords you want to track. Click "Create Alert" to save it. You'll be notified whenever a matching article is published.
How do I search for articles?
You can search for articles using the search bar in the Search News page. Type your keywords and press Enter. You can also use the filters to narrow down results by category, source, date, and status. The search supports full-text search across article titles, summaries, and authors.
What are the different article statuses?
Articles have four statuses:
  • 🔴 Breaking - Urgent news that requires immediate attention
  • ✅ Published - Regular news articles that have been published
  • 📝 Draft - Articles that are still being prepared
  • 📦 Archived - Older articles that have been archived
How do I manage news sources?
You can manage news sources from the News Sources page in the sidebar. Each source has a toggle switch that allows you to enable or disable it. When a source is active, articles from that source will appear in your search results and can trigger alerts. You can also see the number of articles from each source.
Is NewsFlow free to use?
Yes! NewsFlow is completely free to use. We provide a full-featured news monitoring dashboard without any subscription fees. In the future, we may offer premium features, but the core functionality will always remain free.
How do I export articles?
On the Dashboard page, you can export the currently filtered articles by clicking the "Export" button in the results header. This will download a CSV file containing the article titles, categories, sources, publication dates, statuses, and authors.
What happens when I mark all alerts as read?
When you click "Mark all read" in the notification panel, all current breaking and published alerts will be marked as archived. This clears the notification badge and removes them from the active alerts list. You can still find these articles in the search results.
How does real-time news work?
NewsFlow uses WebSocket technology to provide real-time news updates. When the WebSocket server is running, new articles and breaking news are pushed to your dashboard instantly. The connection status is shown in the browser console. If the WebSocket server is not running, NewsFlow falls back to simulated updates.
Where are my preferences and alerts saved?
Your preferences, alerts, and source settings are saved locally in your browser's localStorage. This means your settings persist between sessions, but they are not synced across devices. Clearing your browser cache or using private/incognito mode will remove your saved data.
How do I get help if I'm stuck?
If you need help, you can:
  • Check this Help & Support page for answers to common questions
  • Contact our support team via email at support@newsflow.com
  • Visit our GitHub repository to report issues or suggest features
